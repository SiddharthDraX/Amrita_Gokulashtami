About

Acting is an integral part of India's cultural celebrations during events like Gokulastami (Krishna Janmashtami). It brings to life the stories and legends of Lord Krishna, connecting people with their rich cultural heritage and spirituality. These performances, ranging from dance dramas to folk theater, not only preserve ancient traditions but also showcase India's cultural diversity. They serve as a reminder of India's unity in diversity and have a lasting influence on the country's entertainment industry, with many films drawing inspiration from these cultural narratives. In essence, acting during Gokulastami is a dynamic fusion of tradition, artistry, and modernity, celebrating India's enduring cultural legacy.

For interested students to hone their skills in acting, play-writing and directing, we have staged 150 skits and 2 full length dramas, namely Bon Voyage and Kanchana Seetha, since 2008.
